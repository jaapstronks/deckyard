# Regional Energy Report 2026 — Summary for the Board

Consumption across the four supply regions diverged sharply this year, and the
board asked for a single view of where the load now sits.

## Consumption by region

North region consumed 412 GWh, down from 448 GWh last year, serving 121,000
connections. East region consumed 388 GWh, up from 351 GWh, serving 98,000
connections. South region consumed 522 GWh, up from 495 GWh, serving 143,000
connections. West region consumed 274 GWh, down from 302 GWh, serving 77,000
connections.

## Renewable share over time

The renewable share of total supply has climbed steadily. It stood at 18% in
2021, 24% in 2022, 31% in 2023, 39% in 2024, 46% in 2025, and reached 53% in
2026. The board should note the curve is flattening: the year on year gain has
fallen from 7 percentage points to 7, then 8, then 7, and most recently 7.

## Headline figures

Total supply reached 1,596 GWh. Peak demand hit 2.9 GW on 14 January. Grid
losses ran at 4.1%, the lowest on record. Average residential tariff closed the
year at 0.31 euro per kWh.

## Outlook

Storage capacity is the constraint on further renewable growth. Without the
Eemshaven battery coming online, the renewable share is projected to plateau
near 55% through 2028.
