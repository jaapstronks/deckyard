# Why Our Onboarding Fails — Product Research Findings

We interviewed 34 new customers who abandoned setup in their first week.

## The chain that loses customers

The pattern is causal, and each link produces the next. Our signup form asks for
billing details before the product has demonstrated any value. Because of that,
a large share of users abandon at the billing step. Because they abandon before
reaching the workspace, they never import their first dataset. And because they
never import data, the product has nothing to show them on return, so the
reactivation email lands on an empty account and is ignored.

Put simply: asking for payment early causes abandonment, abandonment causes an
empty workspace, and an empty workspace causes reactivation to fail.

## Four principles for the redesign

The team agreed on four principles. Speed: a new user should see real output
within ninety seconds. Trust: no payment details until value has been shown.
Clarity: one action per screen, never a form with sixteen fields. Recovery: an
abandoned setup should be resumable from the exact step where it stopped.

## What customers told us

One respondent captured the problem better than our own analysis did. "I was
happy to pay you. I just wasn't willing to pay you before I knew whether the
thing worked." — Marieke de Vries, operations lead at a logistics firm.

## Impact if we act

Setup completion currently sits at 41%. Comparable products that defer billing
report completion between 60% and 75%. Closing even half that gap would add an
estimated 1,900 activated accounts per quarter.
