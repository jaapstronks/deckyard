# Digital Intake Programme — Internal Briefing

Zorggroep Noord has run patient intake on paper since 2011. The programme
replaces it with a digital intake used across all fourteen clinics.

## How a referral moves through the new system

The new intake follows five steps, and each one has to complete before the next
begins. First, the referring GP submits the referral through the regional portal.
Second, the intake desk validates the patient's insurance and identity against
the national register. Third, a triage nurse assigns an urgency band, which
determines the target appointment window. Fourth, the scheduling engine offers
the patient three appointment slots by SMS. Fifth, the clinic confirms the
booking and releases the pre-visit questionnaire.

The sequence matters: skipping validation before triage was the single largest
source of rework in the pilot, because a nurse would band a referral that later
turned out to be uninsured.

## Programme milestones

The programme began in March 2024 with a discovery phase across three clinics.
In September 2024 the first pilot went live at the Groningen site. By February
2025 the pilot had extended to four further clinics and the referral portal was
connected to the regional exchange. The full rollout to all fourteen clinics
completed in November 2025. Decommissioning of the paper intake is scheduled for
June 2026, and the final archive migration for December 2026.

## What we learned in the pilot

Average time from referral to confirmed appointment fell from 11.4 days to 3.2
days. The proportion of referrals requiring manual correction dropped from 31%
to 9%. Patient satisfaction with the booking experience, measured on a five
point scale, rose from 3.1 to 4.4.

Staff reported that the SMS slot offer was the single most valuable change,
because it removed the telephone tag that previously consumed most of the
scheduling team's afternoon.
